Resume

I am currently writing a doctoral dissertation in information studies at Åbo Akademi University, where I do research on perceptions of freedom of expression, privacy, and trust in government among librarians and library users. In 2017—2021 I worked as digital scholarship librarian at Uppsala University Library. I have previously worked at IFLA with library and information policy issues.

I hold a Master of Arts in Library and Information Science (hons, dissertation – it’s excellent, DIK says so!) from Uppsala University and a Master of Arts in Digital Asset and Media Management (hons, dissertation) from King’s College London.

About me

I am a librarian, a researcher, and an information advocate. I’m open to freelance work as a lecturer, workshop facilitator, or content producer on topics such as libraries and sustainability (2030 Agenda perspective), copyright and digital creativity in libraries, and privacy in libraries. Send me an e-mail or an owl if you want to talk more.

Generally, I like to do great things (because I’m awesome), small things (I pay attention to detail), or exciting things (I’m an outside-of-the-box thinker, mainly because I was never assimilated by the Borg) or all at the same time. I also make great coffee!

Contact me

For research inquiries or general questions send an e-mail, find my university profile, or connect on Mastodon, Linkedin or Twitter.
